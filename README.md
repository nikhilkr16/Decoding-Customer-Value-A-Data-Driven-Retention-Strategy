## The Question

Is the brand building a loyal customer base, or is it reliant on continuous promotional activity — and what should it do in either scenario?

## The Answer

**The brand has a genuine loyal core, but it is leaking margin by discounting customers who would buy anyway.**

19.4% of customers (758 "Champions") generate 42.2% of total revenue ($1.71M/yr). These customers buy weekly-to-fortnightly, have 33+ previous purchases, and rate the brand 4.1/5.0. They use promos at a 56.3% rate — but their promo dependency score is just 0.056. They are not buying *because* of discounts; they are buying *despite* them and simply taking advantage of available offers.

Meanwhile, 11.2% of the base (436 "Discount Hunters") use promos on 100% of purchases and contribute only 4.4% of revenue. Every dollar they spend costs the brand margin with virtually no loyalty return — their avg previous purchases (12.2) and loyalty score (0.234) are the lowest of any segment.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| **Top 25% of customers = 65.1% of revenue** | Revenue is heavily concentrated; losing Champions is catastrophic |
| **43% of customers use promos, but only 11.2% truly depend on them** | ~32% of promo usage is unnecessary margin loss |
| **Loyalty is driven by frequency, not order size** ($61.50 ICP vs $59.76 avg) | Marketing should optimize for repeat visits, not basket size |
| **Satisfaction predicts loyalty** (4.11 ICP rating vs 3.22 At-Risk) | Post-purchase experience is the highest-leverage investment |
| **13 states show above-avg spend with below-avg promo dependency** | Organic demand exists in IL, AK, TN, LA, GA, NY, AL, WY, AZ, VA, RI, NE, TX |
| **Subscription rate is 1.7× higher among ICPs (45.6% vs 27%)** | Subscription is both a loyalty signal and a loyalty driver |

## Recommended Actions

1. **Sunset promos for Champions** (6-month rollout) — Replace price discounts with loyalty perks (free express shipping, early access). Estimated margin recovery: **$375K–$465K/yr**.

2. **Phase out Discount Hunters** — Accept natural attrition of ~216 pure bargain-chasers (2.2% revenue impact). Attempt to recover ~220 with subscription signals via "earn your discount" model. Net margin recovery: **$10K–$87K/yr**.

3. **Protect Casual Browsers from discount contamination** — This segment (37.3% of base) already buys at full price. Do not introduce promos; invest in subscription conversion instead.

4. **Total estimated margin recovery: $385K–$552K/yr** against a max revenue risk of $175K.

## The Ideal Customer

The brand's most valuable customer buys **37.5× per year**, spends **$2,257 annually**, rates **4.1/5.0**, and subscribes at **45.6%**. They are not defined by age or gender — they are defined by **behavioral consistency**. The strongest acquisition signal is early purchase frequency: customers who buy 2+ times in their first interaction cycle are the most likely future Champions.
